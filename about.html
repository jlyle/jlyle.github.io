How I work

Five things I believe about resumes.

Not vibes. Things I've watched play out hundreds of times from both sides of the desk.

01

A resume is a sales doc, not a memoir.

The reader has 8 seconds and a stack of 80. Your job isn't to be comprehensive — it's to make them stop, slow down, and want to hear more.

"Brevity is the soul of getting a callback."
02

Quantification beats adjectives. Every time.

"Improved performance" is air. "Cut p99 latency 73% on a service handling $2.1B/yr" is a story. The second one gets the phone screen.

03

ATS isn't the enemy. Lazy formatting is.

Every modern parser handles a well-built resume fine. The horror stories come from PDFs with embedded tables, ASCII art borders, and 4-column layouts. Don't.

04

Veterans are systematically undersold.

Almost every transitioning service member I work with has been told they're "too military" by some civilian. They're not. They've just never been taught what to keep, what to translate, and what to drop.

05

The intake call is 60% of the work.

If I get the call right, the first draft is good. If I get the call wrong, no amount of revision saves it. So I record it, listen to it twice, and quote you back to you.

06

One client at a time. Always.

I cap at three active clients in a given week. If you can't get on the calendar, that's why. It's also why my clients tend to land within two months.
